Welcome

The aim of the following data is to shed light on the perception surrounding drug pricing, this is done by taking a dataset of tweets from September 1st and the 23rd 2018 and analysing the content.

Thought Process

  1. To begin, an understanding of the data needs to be had
  2. To achieve this, the nature of the tweets are focusing on has to be laid out
  3. This is done by breaking down what words are used in each tweet and the descriptions of the users to try and find more out about the users
  4. With this in mind, what hashtags were used and what articles were linked gives a great overview on the perception of the pharmaceutical industry
  5. Many tweets were retweets, so seeing who was retweeted and who they are is also an important note

Tweet Analysis

The overall consensus of the most popular tweets suggests that pharmaceutical prices should be lower. This opinion does not seem to be bias towards a certain political leaning, as the most popular retweeted users have a negative view on the pharmaceutical industry. The following tweets are the full length versions of the most common tweets:

  • @realDonaldTrump: Americans deserve to know the lowest drug price at their pharmacy, but “gag clauses” prevent your pharmacist from telling you! I support legislation that will remove gag clauses and urge the Senate to act. #AmericanPatientsFirst
  • @LCNM99: Pfizer CEO gets 61% pay raise—to $27.9 million—as drug prices continue to climb
  • @GraceUnderFire: To b sure you get it, @GOP candidate’s company slashed cancer drug price to RUSSIA by 45% and RAISED it for AMERICANS by 20%
  • @JohnJamesMI: For 18 years @Stabenow has claimed to stand up against high prescription drug costs, while receiving thousands of dollars from them. When Michigan families struggled to pay for the Epipen, Stabenow benefited from Mylan's 400% price increase on the life saving device. #MISen

Conclusion

  • The aim was to get a rough idea of who is discussing these issues and what their ideals were on drug pricing
  • It is clear the opinion that drug and pharmaceutical pricing should be lower is common is unbiased towards political leaning
  • There is a greater presence of conservatism surrounding this issue
  • It is unfair to say that this is with great accuracy as the indicators to this were retweets, word counts and hashtags. Hashtags seemed to have provided political leaning more so than the other because out of context words are not always accurate
